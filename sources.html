All figures in this book come from public data. This appendix documents each source, notes the main caveats, and lists full references (APA style) at the end. Citation keys are given in brackets so they can be reused in the text.

NYSED data files

The enrollment and staffing numbers are built from two New York State Education Department databases, both downloaded from https://data.nysed.gov/downloads.php and distributed as annual Microsoft Access files:

  • Enrollment database (New York State Education Department, 2025a) — the BEDS Day Enrollment table, which gives K-12 enrollment by district. Coverage in this book: school years 2015–16 through 2024–25. Enrollment is the BEDS-day count (first Wednesday of October).
  • Student & Educator (“STUDED”) database (New York State Education Department, 2025b) — the Staff table, which gives teacher, principal, counselor, and social-worker counts plus teacher attendance and turnover. Coverage: school years 2017–18 through 2024–25. Because teacher counts start in 2017–18, any measure that uses teachers (such as students per teacher) begins at school-year-ending

These were stacked into a district-by-year panel by src/build_enrollment_teachers.py; the dataset’s own README records the build in full.

Caveats worth repeating. num_teachers is a district-wide headcount (all grades, not full-time-equivalents), while k12_enrollment is K-12 only. The derived students-per-teacher ratio is therefore an approximate measure for comparison across districts and over time — not NYSED’s official pupil-teacher ratio or average class size. Charter schools are excluded (they have no district-total row), and a small number of districts reorganized within the window (statewide, not in this comparison group).

Cambridge CSD annual financial reports

Cambridge’s audited annual financial statements, for fiscal years ended June 30, 2020 through June 30, 2025, were retrieved from the Federal Audit Clearinghouse (https://app.fac.gov/). The clearinghouse was operated by the U.S. Census Bureau through about 2022 and by the U.S. General Services Administration from 2023 onward — which is why the earlier files (FY2020–FY2022) carry a CENSUS tag and bear the title “Financial statements and independent auditor’s report,” while the later files (FY2023–FY2025) carry a GSAFAC tag and the title “Financial report.” They are the same series of district audits.

  • FY2020 (Cambridge Central School District, 2020), FY2021 (Cambridge Central School District, 2021), FY2022 (Cambridge Central School District, 2022)
  • FY2023 (Cambridge Central School District, 2023), FY2024 (Cambridge Central School District, 2024), FY2025 (Cambridge Central School District, 2025)
