How Cambridge CSD compares with nearby New York school districts

Author

Don Boyd

Published

Overview

This website looks at the Cambridge Central School District (Cambridge CSD, Washington County, New York) alongside nearby districts, using public data from the New York State Education Department (NYSED) and selected other sources. It is one of several analyses in the ccs project; the aim is to put Cambridge’s numbers in context rather than to view them in isolation.

Comparison groups

Different questions call for different peer sets, so this website uses two groups that can be adjusted as the work develops:

  • Graph group (5 districts) — used for charts, where a short list keeps lines legible: Cambridge, Greenwich, Salem (all Washington County), Schuylerville (Saratoga County), and Hoosick Falls (Rensselaer County). These are nearby small districts.
  • Table group (12 districts) — used for tables, which can hold more rows: every Washington County K-12 district plus the two graph-group districts that sit just outside the county (Schuylerville and Hoosick Falls).

The table group is the broader set; the graph group is a readable subset of it. Putnam CSD is left out of the “Washington County K-12” set because it is a K-8 district – it has no high school of its own and pays to send its high-school students elsewhere, so it is not comparable on K-12 measures.

Group Districts
Graph (charts) Cambridge, Greenwich, Hoosick Falls, Salem, Schuylerville
Table (tables) The graph group + Argyle, Fort Ann, Fort Edward, Granville, Hartford, Hudson Falls, Whitehall

Data and a few cautions

The underlying numbers come from NYSED’s public downloads and other reputable sources; each chapter and the Sources appendix give the specifics. A few things to keep in mind throughout:

  • Year labels are school-year-ending years. “2025” means the 2024–25 school year. Enrollment is the BEDS-day count (first Wednesday of October).
  • Teacher counts begin in 2017–18. So time series that depend on teachers (such as students per teacher) start at school-year-ending 2018, even though enrollment runs back to 2015–16.
  • Students per teacher is an approximation, not official class size. It divides K-12 enrollment by a district-wide teacher headcount (all grades, not full-time-equivalents). It is useful for comparison across districts and over time, but it is not NYSED’s official pupil-teacher or average-class-size figure. See the Sources appendix for detail.
  • Charter schools are excluded — they have no district-total row in the source data — so these are district-operated figures.
